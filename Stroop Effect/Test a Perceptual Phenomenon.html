(1) What is the independent variable? What is the dependent variable?

Independent Variable: The Category of the word being congruent word or not.
Dependent Variable: Time taken to recognize the color measured for the both of congruent/incongruent words. I take time as the dependent variable because I believe that time taken can be affected by whether the word is congruent or not.

(2) What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? Specify your null and alternative hypotheses, and clearly define any notation used. Justify your choices.

Null Hypothesis: The average time $\mu$ taken is the same irrespective to whether the word is congruent or not

$$ H_0: \mu_0 >= \mu_a $$
Alternative Hypothesis: The average time taken to recognise incongruent colored text $\mu_a$ is more than that taken for congruent words $\mu_0$

$$ H_a: \mu_0 < \mu_a $$

Since the same person is tested for both congruent and incongruent words, the test is dependent. And I will conduct t-test

Since we testing the mean difference of two samples. I will conduct t-test and I will not assume equality of variances of the populations as there is no indication can be made just from the samples. And below are three approaches to test, one using built-in library, one manual and the final test is by bootstrapping before assuming a t-test, simulating the sampling distribution, then calculating the p-value and t-statistic

I will consider the mean difference as the statistic, and consider a 95% confidency interval one sided test, in other words with no more than a 5% Type I Error tolerance

First Approach: Using a Library¶

In the following tests, I flipped the $ \mu_x , \mu_y $ to get positive values instead of negative ones, so I computed mean of Incongruent - mean of Congruent. Test results absolute values are still the same

Second Approach: Manual test¶

First we would compute the t-statistic using the two means differences t-test and degrees of freed by the equaltions below

$$ t = \frac{(\bar{x}-\bar{y})-(\mu_x - \mu_y)} { \sqrt{ \frac{s_x^2}{n} + \frac{s_y^2}{m} } }$$


$$ df = \frac {( \frac{s_x^2}{n} + \frac{s_y^2}{m} )^2} { \frac{ ( \frac{s_x^2}{n} )^2} {n-1} + \frac{ ( \frac{s_y^2}{m} )^2} {m-1} }$$
In [15]:
x_bar = time_df['Incongruent'].mean()
y_bar = time_df['Congruent'].mean()

x_std = time_df['Incongruent'].std()
y_std = time_df['Congruent'].std()

n_m = len(time_df['Congruent'])

t_num = (x_bar - y_bar) - 0 

t_den = np.sqrt(x_std ** 2 / n_m + y_std ** 2 / n_m )

t_stat = t_num/ t_den

df_num = (x_std ** 2 / n_m + y_std ** 2 / n_m) ** 2
df_den = 1/(n_m - 1) * ( (x_std/n_m) ** 2 + (y_std/n_m) ** 2 )

degrees_of_freedom = df_num / df_den

print('\nt-statistic: ', t_stat, 
      '\n', 'df: ', degrees_of_freedom)
